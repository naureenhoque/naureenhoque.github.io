About

Securing the invisible systems around us

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Information Science & Technologies at Grand Valley State University. My work focuses on wireless communications, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and quantum-resistant systems.

I earned my Ph.D. in Computing and Information Sciences from Rochester Institute of Technology in 2025. My dissertation, Dynamic Defenses to Systematically Secure Exposed Attack Surfaces in Wireless Systems, investigated practical and systematic defenses for vulnerable wireless communication processes.

My research bridges formal security analysis with experimental evaluation using real wireless systems and software-defined radios. I am especially interested in defenses that adapt faster than attackers, including moving target defense, cyber deception, and AI-enabled protection.

Teaching

Teaching interests

I connect foundational concepts with current research, practical systems, and hands-on security analysis.

Cybersecurity

Threat modeling, secure systems, wireless security, network defense, and practical security analysis.

Machine Learning

Core learning methods with attention to robustness, adversarial behavior, and security applications.

Data Security

Protecting data through cryptographic, access-control, privacy, and trustworthy-computing principles.

Computer Networking

Network architecture and protocols, with emphasis on wireless systems, vulnerabilities, and secure design.

Teaching philosophy

I value the fundamental “why” and “how” questions students bring to the classroom. My goal is to help students think critically, experiment confidently, act ethically, and build technology that is secure by design.
